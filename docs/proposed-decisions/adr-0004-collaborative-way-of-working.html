ADR-0004: Collaborative way-of-working

  • Date: 2026-07-17
  • Status: proposed
  • Promoted from: Idea 0004: Collaborative way-of-working
  • Tags: collaboration, method

Context

The decision-trail practice and the josyn substance were built solo. Turning a one-person trail into a team practice needs mechanics — but the danger is over-engineering them. Explicit PRs, issues, and branch protocols are overhead the method's Economy promise cannot afford, and they mistake tooling ceremony for collaboration. The team's real medium is talking.

Two prior decisions frame this. ADR-0002 put the work on GitHub as the reversible sandbox. ADR-0003 settled who accepts a decision — team consensus by reason, with a bounded escalation ladder. This ADR is the operational complement: the day-to-day how, kept deliberately lightweight. The one thing that must not be lightweight is the moment a proposal becomes binding — an ADR must never switch from proposed to accepted silently.

Decision Drivers

  • Talking is the collaboration — the trail records reasoning, it is not itself the review mechanism.
  • Lightweight — no PRs, no GitHub issues, no branch choreography; mechanics cheap enough to actually be followed (the Economy promise).
  • No silent acceptance — the proposed → accepted flip must be a deliberate, visible, attributable act, never slipped in.
  • Portable — rides on plain git primitives, so a switch-back per ADR-0002 loses nothing.
  • No key-person lock-in — the operating role is transferable, supporting the succession aim (Idea 0005).
  • True agile — as a matter of course, the way of working is genuinely agile (adaptive, incremental, feedback-driven), not agile ritual or cargo-cult ceremony.

Considered Options

  • A — Single curator on main, talk as the review, acceptance the one guarded act. One person holds the pen and commits directly to main; the team decides by talking; the only ceremonially-visible transition is accepting an ADR.
  • B — PR-based trail (branches + pull-request review as the gate). A real review surface, but PRs are overhead and cast tooling as the collaboration medium — rejected as too heavy and mis-framed.
  • C — Direct commits with no guarded transition at all. Maximally cheap, but lets an ADR slide to accepted invisibly — the one thing we must prevent.

Why A. It keeps everything cheap and puts the only ceremony exactly where bindingness is conferred. B pays PR overhead for a gate the conversation already provides; C is cheap but loses the no-silent-acceptance guarantee.

Proposed decision

  1. Repo-operator is a role, not a person. One repo-operator / curator holds the pen — the single point that writes to the repo and commits the trail. It is a role, explicitly handed over as needed, never bound to an individual; it starts with the driver. There are no per-artifact owners and no assigned reviewers.

  2. Communication is the collaboration. The team works by talking — in conversation, not through the tooling. The curator records the outcome of those conversations into the trail; what the talk weighed is captured in each ADR's Decision Drivers / Considered Options (ADR-0003, best argument wins). Anyone can raise, shape, or contest a thought by talking; the pen simply serializes the result.

  3. Main-only. No branches, no PRs, no GitHub issues. The curator commits directly to main; each commit is one coherent trail move. With a single operator on a single branch, ordinal collisions and parallel-edit clobbering cannot arise — the curator is the serialization point, so no branch or locking machinery is needed.

  4. The one hard rule — no silent acceptance. proposed → accepted is never a quiet inline edit. Accepting an ADR must be visible three ways at once:

    • a dedicated, atomic commit that does nothing else, message accept ADR-NNNN;
    • the status flip and heading rename together in that commit (## Proposed decision → ## Decision);
    • an acceptance record in the ADR: the date, that team consensus was reached (or which escalation rung per ADR-0003 was used), and who accepted.

    Every other transition — drafting an idea, editing a proposal, writing or advancing a plan — stays a free-flowing lightweight commit. Acceptance is the only guarded act.

  5. True agile, as a matter of course. The way of working is genuinely agile — adaptive, incremental, and feedback-driven — not agile ritual or cargo-cult ceremony. Any thread stays refinable or overthrowable at any time (the method's Agility promise); we optimise for real feedback over process theatre.

Consequences

  • Collaboration cost drops to near zero: no PRs, no issues, no branches — just talking and a curator recording the trail on main.
  • The trail cannot silently harden: an ADR reaching accepted always leaves a dedicated commit, a heading rename, and a named acceptance record, so the binding moment is greppable and honest — implementing ADR-0003's team acceptance concretely.
  • Because the mechanics ride on plain git, a switch-back per ADR-0002 loses no method substance.
  • The single-operator model has an availability cost (the trail advances only when the curator writes), accepted deliberately in exchange for simplicity; the role's hand-over-ability keeps it from becoming key-person lock-in (Idea 0005).
  • This ADR sets the operating model; finer texture (how hand-over of the repo-operator role is signalled, what a "coherent trail move" looks like in edge cases) is refined by talking as the practice matures.
