ADR-0005: Roles, responsibilities & staffing

  • Date: 2026-07-17
  • Status: proposed
  • Promoted from: Idea 0010: Roles, responsibilities & staffing
  • Tags: roles, responsibility, people, staffing

Context

A team practice needs to name who is responsible for what — and, concretely, who fills which role. Two forces pull against each other: stakeholders need a single point of accountability, while the culture is explicitly by reason, not by boss (ADR-0003) and the collaboration medium is talking, not hierarchy (ADR-0004). Without named roles, responsibility diffuses until no one owns progress or the stakeholder relationship; with the wrong naming, the driver quietly becomes a line manager and the non-hierarchical intent collapses.

The concrete situation: responsibility cumulates in the driver, who reports to stakeholders, maintains project-progress, and keeps the whole coherent — yet is not a team-lead by title, even though in practice he will act like one. The starting team is small and known, so the ADR also records the concrete staffing — while treating that line-up as a mutable snapshot, since roles are stable but the people in them may change during the ride.

Decision Drivers

  • Single point of accountability — stakeholders have one address for the project.
  • Accountability without hierarchy — the driver is not a team-lead; decisions stay by reason (ADR-0003).
  • No diffusion — every role has a named in-scope / out-scope boundary.
  • Consistent with ADR-0004 — roles compose with, and are separable from, the repo-operator role.
  • Hand-over-ready — roles are named as roles, so they can transfer (Idea 0005).
  • Staffing is mutable — the concrete line-up must be changeable without re-deciding the role model.

Considered Options

  • A — Named roles with one accountable driver, plus a recorded-but-mutable staffing snapshot. Accountability concentrates in a single non-hierarchical role; the rest of the team contributes by reason with named scope; concrete names are recorded and freely updated.
  • B — Collective ownership, no single accountable role. True to "team by talking", but leaves stakeholders without one address and lets progress-keeping fall between chairs.
  • C — Make the driver a formal team-lead with line authority. Simple accountability, but installs the hierarchy the culture explicitly rejects (ADR-0003).

Why A. It gives stakeholders a single accountable role while keeping authority out of the mechanism, and captures today's line-up without freezing it. B has no single address; C buys accountability by importing hierarchy.

Proposed decision

  1. The driver — single main responsibility-holder. One role, the driver, carries cumulative responsibility. It is accountable for: reporting to stakeholders, maintaining project-progress, and keeping the whole coherent ("keeping it all together"). It is a role, hand-over-able, not a person.

  2. Not a team-lead — accountability without hierarchy. The driver holds no line or disciplinary authority. Decisions remain team consensus by reason with the ADR-0003 escalation ladder; the driver does not get a command shortcut around it. "Acts like a lead" means caretaking and integration, never command.

  3. Separable from the repo-operator. The driver currently also holds the repo-operator / curator role (ADR-0004), but the two are distinct, independently hand-over-able roles.

  4. Roles for the other members.

    • Senior — the experienced architect and JobSystem-Maintainer. In-scope: deep domain authority, safeguarding existing substance, reasoning on architecture.
    • Associate architects — the two younger architects, on an ownership track (growing toward taking roles over, feeding Idea 0005). In-scope: full contribution by reason plus deliberate growth into ownership.
    • All members are contributors: expected to bring domain substance and to shape and contest decisions by talking. Out-scope for non-driver roles: stakeholder reporting and repo operation — the driver's, unless explicitly handed over.

  5. Concrete staffing — a mutable snapshot. As of this ADR:

    • driver → CKL
    • senior (experienced architect, JobSystem-Maintainer) → ORE
    • associate architects (ownership track) → PKL and BWE (tentative at this early point)

    This staffing is explicitly changeable during the ride: updating who fills a role is a lightweight edit to this line-up, not a re-decision of the role model — it does not require a new ADR, only the curator recording the change. Role definitions change through the normal ADR flow; role occupancy does not.

  6. Scope lines are explicit but light. A role's in/out-scope boundary is named when it matters and refined by talking — no exhaustive RACI up front.

Consequences

  • Stakeholders address one accountable role; progress and coherence always have an owner, so responsibility cannot diffuse.
  • Because the driver holds no line authority, accountability does not become hierarchy, keeping ADR-0003's "by reason" intact — the cost is that the driver carries weight he cannot command away, relying on conviction (Idea 0002).
  • The recorded staffing makes today's line-up legible, while its declared mutability keeps the trail honest as people change — and separating role definition from role occupancy means routine staffing changes stay cheap.
  • Naming roles as separable, hand-over-able roles makes the succession hand-over (Idea 0005) mechanical rather than personal, and clarifies who maintains progress under Idea 0009.
  • The associate architects name is a working label; if a better neutral term emerges, it is changed by talking. The PKL/BWE assignment is tentative and expected to firm up as the team forms.
