ADR-0008: Beacon vs. substance guardrail

  • Date: 2026-07-18
  • Status: proposed
  • Promoted from: Idea 0007: Beacon vs. substance guardrail
  • Tags: agentic, guardrail, reversibility

Context

The project carries two goals in tension: it is both the deliverable itself and a beacon / experimental field for agentic ways of working. Without an explicit fence, the "experimental field" ambition can quietly erode the deliverable — and a damaged deliverable would discredit the whole agentic case with exactly the stakeholders we need to convince (ADR-0006).

The guardrail is therefore: experimenting must not let the project substance suffer. The seed idea left four open questions, which this ADR settles:

  • How are experiments fenced from the deliverable substance?
  • What counts as "substance at risk", and who judges it?
  • How do we separate experiment from deliverable in practice (branches, repos, gates)?
  • How is a failed experiment rolled back without collateral damage?

This ADR decides the guardrail principle and mechanism; concrete gate configuration and sandbox choices are refined as the practice matures.

Decision Drivers

  • A damaged deliverable discredits the agentic case with the very stakeholders we must convince — the deliverable's integrity is the non-negotiable.
  • The experimental ambition is genuine and must stay possible — the fence must protect substance without killing experimentation.
  • The deliverable already lives on a portable, reversible footing (ADR-0002) — the guardrail should reuse that, inventing nothing new.
  • "At risk" must be sharp enough to gate on, and someone must own the call (ADR-0003).

Considered Options

  • A — Isolate by default, earn-in through gates. Experiments cannot mutate authoritative deliverable state until they pass review/CI gates; the deliverable's source of truth stays protected, and an experiment crosses in only by proving itself. Isolation is cheap because the deliverable is already portable/reversible.
  • B — Experiment in place, clean up reactively. Run experiments directly against the deliverable and fix damage if it appears. Fast, but it inverts the guardrail — substance is exposed first and defended second, exactly the erosion the fence exists to prevent.
  • C — Fully parallel experiment track. A permanently separate world for all experimentation. Maximally safe but heavy — it slows both tracks and duplicates effort even for trivially reversible experiments.

Why A. It protects substance by construction while keeping experimentation cheap, reusing the portability/reversibility already in place. B defends substance too late; C over-pays for safety on experiments that were never a threat.

Proposed decision

  1. Experiments are isolated by default; the deliverable is earned into, not default-mutated. An experiment may not hold or mutate authoritative deliverable state until it has passed the gates. The deliverable's source of truth — repo docs and code, on the portable side per ADR-0002 — is protected; an experiment enters the deliverable only through review, never by default.

  2. "Substance at risk" = anything that degrades the deliverable's correctness, integrity, credibility, or the guaranteed return path. The judge is the ADR-0003 governance ladder — the driver/co-driver make the call, with the escalation ladder as backstop. Ambiguous cases are treated as substance (precautionary default): when in doubt, fence it.

  3. Separation is mechanical and gate-bounded. Reversible in-repo experiments live on branches behind a PR + green-CI gate; anything that could contaminate authoritative state goes to a separate sandbox/repo. Experiment artifacts sit in docs/intermediate-artifacts/ or a sandbox until promoted. The merge gate is the boundary between experiment and deliverable.

  4. A failed experiment is dropped, not cleaned up. Because the deliverable stays portable and reversible (ADR-0002) and experiments are isolated pre-merge, a failure is discarded by deleting its branch/sandbox with no collateral; anything already merged is git-revertable from history. No experiment may hold authoritative state that cannot be cleanly reverted — that is the precondition for running it at all.

Consequences

  • The deliverable's integrity is protected by construction, so experimentation can stay bold without threatening the beacon's credibility — the trade is a gate (review/CI) between an experiment and the deliverable.
  • Precautionary defaulting will occasionally fence something that was actually safe; accepted deliberately as cheaper than repairing eroded substance.
  • The guardrail reuses the portability/reversibility of ADR-0002 and the governance ladder of ADR-0003; it adds a stance, not new machinery.
  • This ADR decides the principle. Concrete gate configuration (which CI checks, when a separate repo is warranted, promotion runbooks) is left to follow-up decisions and their plans.
