ADR-0009: Relationship to the josyn field

  • Date: 2026-07-18
  • Status: proposed
  • Promoted from: Idea 0008: Relationship to the josyn field
  • Tags: migration, governance, inheritance

Context

This collaborative reset does not start from scratch. Two bodies of prior work already exist and carry real substance:

  • the sealed PoC josyn-platform — authoritative architectural reference for JOSYN (45 ADRs, plans, sanity protocol; no code), with backend + scheduling mature and the surface / JRP / Gateway stream still in transition;
  • the consolidation repo josyn-builder — a one-man decision-trail "migration workshop" (27 ADRs, mostly accepted) meant to re-decide the platform and then disappear, carrying the target repo-world and classification vocabulary, with unfinished migration threads.

The reset must neither waste that substance nor blindly inherit it past the team's scrutiny. This ADR is the bridge from the human/method concerns to the technical field: it settles the stance toward inherited work — what is inherited versus re-openable, how inheritance is ratified into genuine team ownership, and how the builder's unfinished migration continues. It does not make the technical re-decisions themselves (backend untangling, latecomer-repo review, surface/Gateway resolution); those are downstream ideas, ADRs, and plans. The two skim-level source inventories under docs/intermediate-artifacts/ feed this axis.

Decision Drivers

  • Much is already decided and much gold exists — re-deciding it from scratch would waste substance and stall the reset.
  • A culture of adoption by conviction (ADR-0006) forbids silent import — the team must genuinely own what it inherits.
  • The prior repos are read-only sources on the portable side of ADR-0002 — mine them, don't extend them in place.
  • Continuation of unfinished work must itself embody the collaborative way-of-working (ADR-0004), not a solo hand-off.
  • The bar to re-open settled work must be a substantive objection, not blanket re-litigation, or nothing ever settles.

Considered Options

  • A — Presumptive inheritance + cluster ratification + collaborative continuation here. Inherit the durable gold by default, re-home and batch-ratify it by cluster for a genuine team acceptance moment, and continue the builder's live threads collaboratively in this repo, treating both prior repos as read-only sources.
  • B — Re-decide everything from scratch. Maximum team ownership, but it wastes the existing gold and stalls the reset for months re-litigating settled substance.
  • C — Blanket silent inheritance. Fast, but the team never genuinely owns the decisions — it inverts adoption-by-conviction and stores up later dissent.

Why A. It captures the gold without waste, gives the team a real (not ceremonial, not exhaustive) acceptance moment, and makes the continuation itself an instance of the collaboration goal. B wastes substance; C manufactures unearned consensus.

Proposed decision

  1. Presumptive inheritance — nothing silently frozen, nothing silently kept. The durable gold is inherited as-is (adopt, don't re-litigate): the decision-trail method; the repo-world taxonomy / naming / strictly-tiered DAG / SemVer / pinned-platform manifest; the classification vocabulary; the platform-core constraints; the JobSystem-Next whitepaper; and the mature josyn-platform architecture (backend, scheduling). Re-openable are the PoC scaffolding and transitional / unfinished pieces — agentic pilot-seat placement, MVP surface hacks, the unresolved surface / Gateway transition, and builder-specific plumbing — plus anything a team member re-opens with a reasoned trigger. Inherited ≠ hidden: everything stays visible and challengeable; the bar to re-open settled gold is a substantive objection, not blanket re-decision.

  2. Ratify by review, in clusters — no adoption by silent import. Inherited decisions are re-homed into this repo's trail and given the team a genuine acceptance moment, but batch-ratified by cluster (the inventories' A–F and A–D groupings) rather than re-deriving each ADR. A ratification ADR records "we reviewed and adopt cluster X as-is"; anyone may pull a specific inherited ADR out of the batch for genuine re-decision. Ownership comes from review and assent, consistent with ADR-0006 — not decree.

  3. Continue the builder's migration collaboratively, here — not as a solo hand-off. The josyn-builder workshop was always meant to disappear; both it and the sealed PoC are treated as read-only sources (portable, per ADR-0002) — mined, not extended. Their live threads — the Tier-2 requirements-driven re-start simulation, the still-proposed ADRs (0023 commons-helpers, 0027 agent-guidance), the open seed ideas, latecomer-repo quality review, and backend untangling — are re-hosted as ideas / ADRs / plans in this repo and picked up by the team under ADR-0004. The continuation itself becomes an instance of the collaboration goal.

Consequences

  • The gold is carried forward without waste, and the team gets a real (not exhaustive) ownership moment — the trade is the up-front effort of cluster ratification before the technical work resumes.
  • Presumptive inheritance with a substantive-objection bar keeps the reset moving, at the cost that a weakly-held inherited decision may pass unless someone actively re-opens it.
  • Treating the prior repos as read-only sources preserves reversibility and a clean provenance, but means their unfinished threads must be re-hosted here before they can advance — nothing is edited in place upstream.
  • This ADR decides the stance; the concrete ratification batches (which clusters, in what order) and the re-hosted technical re-decisions are follow-up ideas, ADRs, and plans. Their sequencing belongs to the project's phase / milestone framing in Idea 0009: Project progress.
