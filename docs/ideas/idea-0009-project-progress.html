Idea 0009: Project progress

  • Date: 2026-07-17
  • Status: promoted
  • Parent: Idea 0001: Found the collaborative, decision-driven agentic way of working
  • Promoted to: ADR-0010: Project progress
  • Tags: process, agile, milestones

The axis

Project progress must be transparently defined — carried in explicit phases / milestones, agile in spirit: visible, incremental, and reviewable rather than an opaque march. Anyone should be able to see where we are, what a milestone requires, and what comes next. This is the progress-mechanics face of the true agile way of working set in ADR-0004.

Why it matters

Transparent phasing is what makes the experimental way of working legible to the organization and steerable by the team. Milestones give the project reviewable checkpoints, and they are the natural home for criteria that gate big moves — including the guaranteed switch-back to Azure DevOps from ADR-0002.

Open questions (seed)

  • What are the phases / milestones, and how are they made transparent (where do they live, how are they tracked, kept portable per ADR-0002)?
  • What does "agile" concretely mean here — cadence, increments, review rhythm?
  • The Azure DevOps switch-back as a milestone criterion: at which milestone (or under which conditions) does the switch become due? This is where its trigger is planned — deliberately not planned now, only surfaced.
  • How do milestone criteria get defined, reviewed, and marked met?
