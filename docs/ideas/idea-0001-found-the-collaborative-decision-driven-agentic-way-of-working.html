Idea 0001: Found the collaborative, decision-driven agentic way of working

  • Date: 2026-07-16
  • Status: decomposed
  • Tags: founding, culture, change-management, succession, agentic, decision-driven, governance, collaboration

This is the founding idea for the project. It is deliberately broad and captured without deep investigation — a starting position now decomposed into child ideas (see Decomposition at the end). The parent stays as the map.

What this idea is really about. Not the technical platform. The subject is people, culture, psychology, method, policy, and collaborative way-of-working — the shift of a team into an agentic, decision-driven culture. The existing josyn work (the sealed PoC josyn-platform and the consolidation repo josyn-builder) is the field this plays out on — the substance, not the subject.

Background

  • A new project in the company is in the start-position.
  • A lot of groundwork was already done by an individual driver; there is much to bring in and much already done.
  • There is a PoC (josyn-platform) carrying much value, many decisions, and carried-out plans — now logically sealed.
  • There is a follow-up decision-trail repo (josyn-builder) consolidating the PoC — still not collaborative — also holding many decisions and carried-out plans.
  • We do not start "from scratch".

The shift

  • The major change: from individual speed-boat to a collaborative project.
  • This means a reset — and a need to re-decide "all" — but by the team, not by decree.

The driver's stance

  • The driver is team-lead and senior developer; originator of both JobSystem and the decision-trail method.
  • It is explicitly not about "me". The person must become replaceable.
  • Some core ideas are non-negotiable — agentic and decision-driven — but the aim is to convince, not to rule.
  • Succession is a goal: when the work is done, the driver wants to step back and let the "new generation" take over and own it.

Culture

  • Decisions by reason, not by boss.
  • The driver can have the last word, but does not want to overrule teammates or leave them unconvinced.

Team & stakeholders

  • 3 young, clever teammates — architect-material; the intended new-generation owners.
  • 4 technical leads (one of them the "leads-lead") and a department head — peers and authority to bring along and keep aligned; they carry the weight of the traditional way.

Method & tooling

  • Currently traditional: Azure DevOps — work items, pipelines, standard policy.
  • The project's "new way" must run on GitHub, deliberately free of policy-burden, like the PoC — a low-friction experimental field.
  • Reversibility is a hard constraint: a planned migration-switch back from the GitHub playground to the overheaded corporate standard must always remain possible.

Two goals

  • The project itself.
  • The project as a beacon and experimental field for agentic work.

Non-negotiable

  • An agentic and decision-driven way of working.

Guardrail

  • The project substance must not suffer from the "experimenting".

Decomposition

This founding idea braids several orthogonal axes that only carry weight together as a map. On 2026-07-16 it was decomposed: each axis was budded into its own child idea (Parent: 0001); the parent stays as the map, and each child matures and promotes 1:1 to its own ADR.

  • Idea 0002: Adoption by conviction — winning genuine buy-in for the non-negotiables, across young architects and peers/authority.
  • Idea 0003: Decision culture & governance — "by reason, not by boss"; decision-trail as the team's shared instrument.
  • Idea 0004: Collaborative way-of-working — one-man → team mechanics: roles, ownership, review, hand-offs.
  • Idea 0005: Succession & replaceability — founder-exit by design; new-generation ownership.
  • Idea 0006: Tooling sandbox & reversibility — GitHub low-policy field ↔ guaranteed switch-back to Azure DevOps.
  • Idea 0007: Beacon vs. substance guardrail — experimental field for agentic work without harming deliverable substance.
  • Idea 0008: Relationship to the josyn field — inherit vs. re-open the existing platform/builder substance.

Why these axes: each is separately decidable (each can become one ADR), but none captures the whole; together they map the shift from an individual speed-boat to a collaborative, agentic, decision-driven culture. "Convince, not rule" (0002) also threads through the others as a value, but is kept as its own axis because winning buy-in is itself work with its own decisions.
