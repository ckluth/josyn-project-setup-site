Idea 0002: Adoption by conviction

  • Date: 2026-07-16
  • Status: seed
  • Parent: Idea 0001: Found the collaborative, decision-driven agentic way of working
  • Tags: culture, change-management, psychology

The axis

The non-negotiables — agentic and decision-driven — must be adopted by genuine conviction, not decree. The task is to win buy-in, across different audiences:

  • the 1 experienced architect-mate to fully keep in the loop,
  • the 2 young architects (the possible future owners, to empower), and
  • the 4 technical leads (incl. the "leads-lead") and the department head (peers and authority, carrying the weight of the traditional way, to convince and keep aligned).

Why it matters

The driver can have the last word but does not want anyone overruled or left unconvinced. A culture adopted by reason is the only kind that survives the founder's eventual exit — imposed practice would collapse the moment he steps back.

Open questions (seed)

  • What actually convinces each audience — evidence, demonstration, participation?
  • How to demonstrate value early without over-promising?
  • What to do with a peer who stays genuinely unconvinced?
  • What is the right pace and sequence of adoption?
